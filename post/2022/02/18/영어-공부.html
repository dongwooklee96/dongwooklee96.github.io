스크립트

In addtion to cancer's effects on people's physical health, it can also create new and unfamiliar emotions.
A cancer dignnosis can also intensify existing feelings.
What's more, these feelings may change daily, hourly, or even minute to minute.
This is ture for peope who are currently in treatment, who are done with treatment, or who are friends or family members of people with caner.
Feeling overwhelmed is one of the most common reactions to cancer diagnosis.
People wonder how they're going to live a normal life.
They fear that their routines will be disrupted by doctor visits and treatments.
To avaoid this feeling, ask your doctor question and don't be afraid to say when you don't understand.

Anger is another emotion frequently associated with a cancer dagnosis.
People may feel anger or resentment towards their healthcare providers, their healthy friends, or even towards their loved ones.
If you feel angry, don't pretend that everything is okay.
It's not healty to keep anger inside you.
Talk with your family and friends about your anger.

Finally, there's fear. It's scary to learn that you have caner. What's more, people fear the pain of cancer and of its treatment.
People also worry about taking care of their family or keeping their job.
To cope with this fear, it helps to be informed.
Most people's fear subsides when they learn more detail about their situation.
